Basketball

The first sport I was introduced to was football, and while I did enjoy it; it was just never something that I wanted to do.

I honestly cannot remember when I first watched basketball. It had something to do with watching clips of the 90s Chicago Bulls. I almost instantly fell in love and it became a huge part of my life.

I played from elementary, all through middle school, and the first few years of high school. However, I had a lingering back injury that I never got checked out, and it would cause me to miss a lot of games. Once I did get it checked out, I was told that I had fractured my spine in a few differnt places and would need to have surgery. The surgery went well and I was able to recover relatively well, but my competitive basketball career was over for the forseeable future. I still shoot around every now and then, but it is no longer one of the main focuses of my life.

Writing

Somewhere in the middle of my basketball phase, I found out that I had an interest in writing.

During the fourth grade I was asked to participate in an academic writing competition. I agreed but I never really took it seriously, even while doing the competition it wasn't on the front of my mind. However, to my surprise, I won first place in the contest. That's when I started to like it more and take it more seriously.

Throughout high school I continued to write for academic competitions, and now I still do it from time to time just as a hobby.

Programming

The first time I was introduced to programming was when I was put into computer science during my first year of high school.

I didn't really find it that interesting though until my junior year of high school, I started with Java, learning the basics and writing some basic programs. When I went to college we started with python, then moved into some C++, but we never really got too deep into either.

After spending a year in college I decided to go another route and I started trying to find a career in web development.
